07. System Parameters

System Analysis

The TM5-700 cobot features a 5 kg payload capacity and a 700 mm working reach, integrated on the LD-250 mobile platform (250 kg payload). The custom pallet is 3D-printed from ABS, featuring a 230 mm × 230 mm footprint, 8×8 grid top, 26.5 mm fork-entry clearance, 19 mm side walls, and a 53 mm RFID slot.

Section 01

Material Selection for Gripper

🎯 Objectives

  • ✓ Evaluate the structural performance of candidate 3D printing materials for the gripper.
  • ✓ Compare stress, displacement, strain, and factor of safety under identical loading conditions.

🛠️ Candidate Materials

  • PLA Polylactic Acid (NatureWorks Ingeo 4032D)
  • PETG Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol (Eastman Amphora AM1800)
  • ABS Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (SABIC CYCOLAC MG47)
  • ASA Acrylonitrile Styrene Acrylate (Luran S797S)

👁️ Key Observations

  • PLA: Highest elastic modulus of 3500 MPa, translating to the lowest deformation and highest stiffness under load.
  • PETG: High toughness and ductility, resulting in better overall impact resistance.
  • ABS: Offers a highly balanced strength-to-toughness profile, making it a robust choice for mechanical performance.
  • ASA: Performs similarly to ABS but with significantly enhanced UV and environmental resistance.

🧮 Structural Safety Calculation

The Factor of Safety (FOS) calculation evaluates structural integrity under loading by dividing each material's yield strength by the maximum simulated von Mises stress.

FOS =
Yield Strength (σyield) Max von Mises Stress (σmax)

Stress & Safety Margin: The maximum von Mises stress was approximately 1.05–1.06 MPa for all candidate materials. Based on yield values, the resulting structural Factor of Safety is exceptionally high, with PLA achieving the safest margin of 56.60.

Displacement Observation: PLA exhibited the lowest deformation (0.117 mm) due to its high elastic modulus (3500 MPa), while PETG showed the highest deformation (0.215 mm) due to its lower stiffness.

Strain Observation: PLA experienced the lowest strain (0.000201), indicating greater rigidity, whereas PETG exhibited the highest strain (0.000375) reflecting higher ductility. ABS and ASA showed moderate values.

Outcome

Final Material Selection: PLA (NatureWorks Ingeo 4032D)

Material selection was based on both FEA simulation results and practical manufacturing considerations. PLA 4032D exhibited superior structural performance with lower deformation and strain compared to the other candidate materials. Furthermore, the availability of PLA filament for immediate fabrication supported its selection. Hence, PLA 4032D was chosen as the final material for the end-effector prototype.

  • ✓ Lowest displacement in both loading conditions.
  • ✓ Lowest strain among all candidate materials.
  • ✓ Highest Factor of Safety (FOS) (~56.60 in Case 1, ~58.25 in Case 2).
  • ✓ Highest yield strength (60 MPa) for load-bearing capacity.
Section 02

Solenoid Selection

Holding Force & Solenoid Calculation

📦 Pallet Assembly Component Weights

Component Unit Weight × Qty Total Weight
Pallet Weight 428 g × 1 428 g
RFID Tag 15 g × 1 15 g
Mild Steel Plates 68 g × 2 136 g
Solenoids 60 g × 4 240 g
Supported Pallet Assembly Load Pallet+RFID+Plates 579 g (0.579 kg)

Note: Although the solenoids themselves weigh 240 g, they are mounted on the gripper body and do not contribute to the lifted pallet assembly load (WTotal = 579 g) that they must actively hold.

🧮 Holding Force Calculations

  • Load acting on pallet assembly (W): (428 + 15 + 136) g = 579 g = 0.579 kg
  • Operating Load (F = m × g): 0.579 × 9.81 = 5.68 N
  • Considering Design FOS = 2: Fdesign = 2 × 5.68 = 11.36 N
  • Load per Solenoid (Fsolenoid): 11.36 / 4 = 2.84 N
  • Convert to kgf (Fsolenoid / 9.81): 2.84 / 9.81 = 0.29 kgf
💡 Practical Selection & Real-world Margins

Theoretical calculations provide the minimum requirement of 0.29 kgf per electromagnet. Electromagnets are highly sensitive to air gaps and surface conditions, so the actual holding force can be considerably lower than the rated value. To account for these practical factors and ensure reliable operation, a 5 kgf solenoid was selected.
