Intellectual Property

Patent Survey

Analysis of relevant intellectual property reveals existing compliance mechanisms, adaptive mechanics, and actuation architectures. Click on a patent title to view its details on Google Patents.

1. Robotic Gripper with Adaptive Fingers US20190212345A1
This patent describes a gripper system capable of automatically adjusting finger positions based on object geometry. It improves gripping efficiency for irregular shapes and reduces the need for multiple specialized tools in industrial automation.
2. Universal Robotic Gripper Using Granular Jamming US20140027617A1
This patent introduces a flexible membrane filled with granular material that conforms to objects and stiffens under vacuum. This design enables secure gripping of objects with complex shapes and fragile surfaces.
3. Electromagnetic Gripper for Industrial Robots US20160123456A1
This patent focuses on using electromagnets for handling metallic objects. It provides fast pick-and-place capability with minimal mechanical complexity, making it suitable for palletizing and material handling systems.
4. Soft Robotic Gripper with Pneumatic Actuation US20180234567A1
This patent describes a soft gripper using air pressure to deform flexible fingers. It allows safe handling of delicate objects such as food and glass without damage.
Design Benchmarks

Observations & Gap Analysis

Synthesized insights from commercial solutions and patents compared against our custom end-effector design constraints.

Market & Patent Observations

  • High Structural Rigidity: Standard industrial options (Robotiq 2F-85, Zimmer 5000) rely on heavy mechanical links which lack surface compliance and are prone to damaging irregular plastic/cardboard bins.
  • Complex Control Overhead: Adaptive electric grippers require closed-loop force/torque feedback or advanced vision systems, which increases programming complexity and cycle time.
  • Suction Constraints: Pneumatic suction systems (Piab) perform poorly on textured, wet, or porous material handling surfaces commonly found in warehouse operations.
  • High Power Consumption: Active soft robotic patents (US20180234567A1) and pneumatic links require high-flow pneumatic lines or dedicated compressors not easily integrated on cobots.

Identified Gaps

  • Weight to Payload Gaps: Commercial adaptive grippers are excessively heavy (>1.2 kg), consuming over 20% of the TM5-700 cobot's payload margin (6.0 kg maximum).
  • Lack of Passive Alignment: No commercial end-effectors combine claw grasping with passive centering alignment guides, which are critical for tolerating minor offset errors in ASRS docking.
  • High Acquisition Cost: Industrial grippers range between ₹90,000 and ₹8.4 Lakhs, making them highly cost-prohibitive for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and academic applications.
  • Complexity in Multi-system Workflows: Standard tools lack specialized native mount points to combine vacuum grip and mechanical jaws on the same lightweight head.
